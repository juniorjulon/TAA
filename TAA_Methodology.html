Pillar I — Fundamentals
Leading indicators of economic and corporate health. These signals lead markets by 1–6 months. They tell you where the economy is going, not where it's been. Use rolling 5-year z-scores for most; monthly PMI data needs only 60-month windows.
Sign convention for Fixed Income: A strong fundamental signal (PMI rising, GDP revising up) is + for equity but − for duration (rates rise → bond prices fall). Multiply these signals by −1 before including them in FI pillar scores. Credit (IG/HY/EM) uses the equity sign since spread tightening = growth = positive.

Pillar II — Momentum
Price trends and spread dynamics. These signals exploit the persistence of returns across asset classes. A key insight: for multi-asset TAA, the 12-1 month price momentum (skip last month) is the single highest-IC signal. Spread momentum for fixed income mirrors price momentum for equity.

Pillar III — Sentiment
Market-implied fear, positioning, and liquidity conditions. Most sentiment signals are contrarian — extreme fear (high VIX, high PCR) is often a buy signal for equity; extreme complacency (low VIX) is a sell signal. This pillar has the highest information ratio at turning points but is the noisiest in normal regimes.
Key sentiment signals currently TBD in Excel: VIX, MOVE, TED spread, PCR, USD strength. These need to be added from FRED (free) or Bloomberg before this pillar is operational. Without sentiment, cap pillar weight at 0% and redistribute to other pillars.

Pillar IV — Valuation
Long-term anchor signals. Valuation has a low information ratio in the short run (assets can stay expensive/cheap for years) but high information ratio over 3–5 year horizons. Use as a conviction filter: valuation confirms or contradicts the other three pillars. The Equity Risk Premium is the most important cross-asset valuation signal.

Four FI asset classes with their specific pillar compositions. Note: for all FI, macroeconomic growth signals are inverted (strong growth → higher rates → bond prices fall). Credit (Corp + EM) is the exception for the spread component: spread tightening driven by growth is positive.
Equity — Signal Blueprint
Equity asset classes with sub-class detail. US Equity includes style tilts (Growth vs Value vs Quality) and sector rotation. All signals are signed positively with respect to equity performance.
Aggregation Logic
Step-by-step process from raw series to composite z-score. This is the engine that runs every time you update the data.
1
Normalize each series → z-score
Every raw series becomes a rolling z-score. Window varies by signal type: 60 months for monthly PMI, 252×5 days for daily spread/price signals, 252×10 days for valuation signals. Always winsorize at ±3σ. Use EWMA (λ=0.95) as the production default — it handles regime breaks naturally.
def rolling_zscore(s, window, ewma=True, lam=0.95): if ewma: mu = s.ewm(span=window).mean() sigma = s.ewm(span=window).std() else: mu = s.rolling(window).mean() sigma = s.rolling(window).std() return (s - mu).div(sigma).clip(-3, 3)
2
Apply sign conventions before aggregating
Some signals must be inverted before entering FI pillars. Growth/PMI are positive for equity but negative for duration. Spread changes are inverted (tightening = positive for credit). Contrarian sentiment signals are inverted at extremes.
# For FIXED INCOME (duration): macro signals inverted z_pmi_fi = -1 * z_pmi_ism z_gdp_fi = -1 * z_gdp_revision_us z_cesi_fi = -1 * z_cesi_us # For CREDIT: spread tightening is positive z_oas_mom = -1 * rolling_zscore(oas_change_1m) # For SENTIMENT (contrarian at extremes): vix_pctile = VIX.rolling(252*5).rank(pct=True) z_vix_eq = np.where(vix_pctile>0.85, +1.5, np.where(vix_pctile<0.15, -1.5, -1*rolling_zscore(VIX)))
3
Aggregate within each pillar
For each pillar, compute a weighted average of its signals. The weights reflect each signal's typical information content for that asset class and pillar. Then re-normalize the pillar score as a z-score in its own history (prevents pillar with more signals from dominating).
# Example: Pillar I (Fundamentals) for US Equity pillar_F_us = ( 0.35 * z_pmi_composite_us + # ISM Mfg+Services avg 0.25 * z_cesi_us + # Surprise index 0.20 * z_gdp_revision_us + # GDP forecast change 0.15 * z_eps_growth_us + # EPS growth z-score 0.05 * z_eps_beat_us # Beat % ) # Re-normalize: prevents compounding across iterations Z_F_us = rolling_zscore(pillar_F_us, window=252).clip(-3,3)
4
Combine pillars → composite z-score
Weighted average of the 4 re-normalized pillar scores. Weights differ by asset class: equity uses more momentum, fixed income uses more valuation. The final composite is the primary decision variable.
# Pillar weights by asset class weights = { 'MM': {'F':0.10,'M':0.15,'S':0.25,'V':0.50}, # mostly carry/valuation 'STFI': {'F':0.20,'M':0.25,'S':0.20,'V':0.35}, 'LTT': {'F':0.25,'M':0.25,'S':0.20,'V':0.30}, 'LTCO': {'F':0.20,'M':0.30,'S':0.20,'V':0.30}, 'LTEM': {'F':0.25,'M':0.30,'S':0.20,'V':0.25}, 'USE': {'F':0.25,'M':0.30,'S':0.20,'V':0.25}, 'DM': {'F':0.25,'M':0.30,'S':0.20,'V':0.25}, 'EM': {'F':0.25,'M':0.30,'S':0.20,'V':0.25}, } Z_composite = sum(weights[ac][p] * Z_pillar[p] for p in 'FMSV')

Pillar agreement check
Count how many pillars agree on direction. This multiplier prevents high-conviction calls when only 1 pillar is driving the signal. It's the quality filter of the system.
directions = [np.sign(Z_pillar[p]) for p in 'FMSV' if abs(Z_pillar[p]) > 0.25] majority_dir = max(set(directions), key=directions.count) n_agree = sum(1 for d in directions if d == majority_dir) # Conviction cap by pillar agreement # 4/4 agree → full conviction | 3/4 → 80% | 2/4 → 50% | 1/4 → 0% conviction_mult = {4:1.0, 3:0.8, 2:0.5, 1:0.0}[n_agree]
Absolute vs Relative Views
The composite z-score feeds into two view types. Most institutional TAA systems weight the relative view more heavily (60–70%) than the absolute view, since the benchmark already captures some absolute valuation through its own rebalancing.
Absolute View — Z-composite vs its own history
Absolute view answers: "Is this asset class attractive on its own merits, compared to its own history?" Z_composite > 0 means the asset is above its own historical average quality. You can simultaneously hold absolute OW positions in multiple asset classes if all have positive composites.

Relative View — Cross-sectional ranking within universe
Relative view answers: "Which asset class do I prefer over the others?" Normalize composite scores cross-sectionally: subtract the universe mean, divide by universe standard deviation. This creates a relative ranking where the best asset has z_rel > 0 and the worst has z_rel < 0, even if both have positive absolute scores.
# Step 1: collect all composites for the universe at date t composites = {ac: Z_composite[ac] for ac in universe} # Step 2: cross-sectional normalization mu_cs = np.mean(list(composites.values())) std_cs = np.std(list(composites.values())) Z_rel = {ac: (composites[ac] - mu_cs) / std_cs for ac in composites} # Step 3: combine absolute and relative views alpha = 0.35 # 35% absolute, 65% relative (institutional standard) Z_final = {ac: alpha*composites[ac] + (1-alpha)*Z_rel[ac] for ac in composites} # Step 4: tilt = Z_final × max_tilt × conviction_mult tilt = {ac: np.clip(Z_final[ac] * max_tilt * conv_mult[ac], -max_tilt, max_tilt) for ac in composites}
Practical example: how OW equity / UW FI emerges
Suppose at date T you compute these composite z-scores:

Money Market: Z=+0.3 · STFI: Z=+0.5 · LT Treasuries: Z=−0.2 · LT US Corp: Z=+0.4 · LT EM: Z=+0.6
US Equity: Z=+1.4 · DM ex-US: Z=+0.9 · EM Equity: Z=+1.8

Cross-sectional mean = +0.71 · std = +0.61

Relative z-scores: MM=−0.67 · STFI=−0.34 · LT Tsy=−1.49 · Corp=−0.51 · LT EM=−0.18 · US Eq=+1.13 · DM=+0.31 · EM Eq=+1.77

Result: "OW EM Equity (highest), moderate OW US Equity and DM, UW Long Treasuries and MM in relative terms" — even though MM and Corp have positive absolute z-scores, they are below the universe average, so they get underweighted relative to equity. This is exactly how "OW equity / UW FI" calls are generated in practice.
Special rule: regime override
Crisis override (hard rule): If VIX > 80th percentile AND MOVE > 80th percentile simultaneously → force all conviction multipliers to 0 → all tilts become 0 (neutral). This prevents the signal engine from fighting a systemic flight-to-quality event. The override lifts when both return below their 70th percentiles.
crisis_flag = ( (vix_pctile > 0.80) & (move_pctile > 0.80) ) # Apply to all tilts: final_tilt = raw_tilt * (~crisis_flag).astype(int)
